## 2. What information we collect

### 2.1 Information you provide directly

On the Website or via our internal tools (for example, journalist/admin accounts), we may collect:

* Name and contact information (such as email address) when you contact us or create an account.
* Login credentials for journalist or admin accounts (stored in encrypted form).
* Content you submit (articles, images, videos, comments, etc.).

We do **not** collect passwords for your Facebook account.

### 2.2 Information collected automatically

When you visit the Website, our servers automatically log:

* IP address
* Browser type and version
* Device information
* Date and time of access
* Visited pages and referring URLs

We may also use cookies and similar technologies to:

* remember your preferences (language, theme, etc.);
* secure sessions for logged-in users; and
* analyze basic usage statistics.

You can usually control cookies in your browser settings. If you disable cookies, some features of the Website may not work properly.

### 2.3 Data from Facebook APIs (Page data only)

Our Facebook App uses Meta’s Graph API to access **Page-level data only**, such as:

* Page access token;
* Page ID and name;
* Information about posts published via the app (post IDs, visibility, etc.).

We use these permissions strictly to:

* create and publish posts linking to articles on our Website;
* start and manage live broadcasts from our newsroom to the Page;
* retrieve basic performance or error information about those posts.

We **do not**:

* access personal timelines of individual Facebook users;
* read private messages;
* collect or store personal profile data from users who simply like, follow, or view the Page.
